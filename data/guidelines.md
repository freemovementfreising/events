# Group Agreements & Guidelines

*Please read before joining to see if this space is for you.*

Welcome to the **Free Movement Collective Freising**. This is a space for exploration, connection, and presence — where movement becomes a language for self-awareness and shared experience. Through dance and embodied practices, we invite you to tune into your body, meet others with curiosity, and co-create a mindful, artistic, and experimental environment.

At the heart of this practice is community. Moving together allows us to witness and be witnessed, to learn from each other's expressions, boundaries, and journeys. In this shared space, we cultivate empathy, connection, and a sense of belonging through intentional movement and mutual respect.

To ensure that everyone feels safe and welcome, we have formulated some shared agreements.

---

## Group Agreements

- Take care of yourself and each other. Support the facilitator's and the group's shared well-being. Together, we keep the space safe, inclusive, and respectful.
- We share responsibility to keep the space clean and organized.
- Arrive on time so we can start together.
- Speak from your own experience ("I") instead of generalizing ("one," "we").
- Be curious about other people's experiences. Different and even contradictory truths can coexist.
- Resistance and discomfort can be part of the process. We commit to staying in relationship with what arises — and with each other.
- Be open to trying new things, expressing yourself playfully, and stepping into your learning zone.
- **Confidentiality:** personal revelations stay within the group.

---

## Consent & Boundaries

- Consent is key. Everyone respects their own boundaries and those of others — physically, emotionally, energetically.
- It is always okay to say "no" — without explanation or justification.
- Be aware that we all have different comfort and energy levels, abilities, experiences, and interests.
- If you're unsure whether a movement or touch is welcome, take a moment to check in verbally, visually, or energetically. When in doubt, ask.
- If something feels off, you can always step away.

---

## Agreements Around Dancing

- Practice consent culture: ask before engaging physically, respect boundaries, and remember that "no" is always valid without explanation.
- Avoid gripping or holding your dance partners — especially hands or feet, which are often needed for support and balance.
- Be mindful of sensitive areas such as knees, joints, the face, chest, and pelvic area.
- Keep your hands and face away from your partner's breasts and genitals; avoid drawing others onto your genitals.
- Pay attention to non-verbal signals (e.g., facial expressions, body tension) and adjust accordingly.
- Some dances might seem sensual or erotic, but do not assume that a person wants to have an intimate dance with you.
- You may become sexually aroused during a dance. Do not pursue the arousal — just notice it and let it pass. It may be appropriate to leave the dance.
- **You may leave a dance at any time** — if your partner is physically safe — for any reason or for no reason:
  - You owe no one an explanation for leaving a dance.
  - Leave if you find yourself losing awareness of your own boundaries.
  - Leave if you feel unsafe emotionally or physically.
  - Trust your instincts.
  - To leave, you may use any of the following: move out of the dance, make a gesture, or say "Thank you" or "I am done."
- If you are not dancing, stay at the side of the floor.
- Witnessing (observing from the side) is encouraged — take the opportunity from time to time, as it deepens the practice.
- Nonverbal sounds (humming, sighing, singing, etc.) are welcome.
- Keep spoken contributions short and quiet; conversations only if relevant to well-being or safety. Longer conversations should take place outside the dance room.

---

## Participation & Self-Care

- All guidance is an invitation (e.g., guided exercises): always listen to your own body and do what feels right for you.
- Take breaks whenever you need them. You are welcome to use the sides of the room, the balcony, or briefly step out.
- If you decide to leave the session, please let the facilitator know.

---

## Conflict & Support

- If discomfort, boundary-crossing, discrimination, or crisis occurs, we pause, address it, and give space to those affected.
- The space is adaptable: if something needs attention, we stop and tend to it with care.
- In this space, we center emotional care and allyship. When harm or discomfort arises, we respond by prioritizing the needs of those most affected — offering space, support, and protection from emotional burden. The person harmed leads the pace and depth of engagement, and others listen and support without centering their own emotional needs.

---

## Inclusion & Safer Space

Our priority is to create a safe space for queer, trans, and non-white people. Everyone is welcome if you are ready to give space, listen, and practice allyship.

We do not tolerate behaviour that undermines the dignity or self-esteem of any individual or creates an intimidating, hostile, or harassing environment. Discriminatory speech or conduct — whether racist, sexist, transphobic, or homophobic — is not acceptable, nor is any form of discrimination based on ability, age, class, religion, language, or ethnic origin. Verbal and/or physical aggression is not tolerated. In case of an incident, please contact the facilitator.

We propose using the two restrooms on the dance floor level (3rd floor) as all-gender restrooms.

## Accessibility

- The venue is wheelchair accessible (3rd floor with elevator).
- If you are sensitive to noise, you may bring earplugs or earmuffs. Sometimes we work without music; at other times the music gets louder.
- The session can be held bilingually (German/English) if needed.
- Feel free to let the facilitator know in advance if you have specific needs: [korinna.schmitz@hswt.de](mailto:korinna.schmitz@hswt.de)
